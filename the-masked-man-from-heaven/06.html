Ergodotis dashed over to his mount. "Our monarch will go to battle with the country of the water! Prepare to be surprised! "As soon as he mounted his horse, he said. Then he takes the wheel.

When word got out, the border market erupted into a brawl. Because they were certain that war would break out here, the merchants tidied out their stores swiftly.

It seemed surprising since Ergodotis continued on his way instead of returning. From a distance, he appears to be on his way to the water country's palace.

The guards held Ergodotis, but Evdaimonia, who had just emerged from the palace, ordered him to be released so that they might talk.

"How did your king react?" Evdaimonia inquired.

"He appears to dislike it so much that he wishes for a kingdom-wide conflict."

"War? We'd much sooner apologize and withdraw than–"

"Our people at the border have fallen, your queen!" exclaimed a passing water citizen who had nearly fallen while riding a horse at a fast pace.

"They've also built a fort near the border!" says the narrator. He continued and then walked away. He appears to be one of the warriors guarding the bordering water nation.

After that, Evdaimonia entered the palace and went inside a chamber. A table with old books sat in the room's center, flanked by many shelves of additional volumes.

Agios, that old book was their instruction manual.

Evdaimonia is confident that there is a page somewhere that will be of assistance.

"When two brothers are at odds, We will send an anonymous mediator to assist those who have distributed goodness to mankind," according to Chapter Altheia, the second chapter.

The Queen recalled her husband, Kalos, imprisoning a pundit beneath the palace. At that point, he arrived with the book. "You there. Our country is currently in jeopardy, and I am requesting your assistance."

"I have no desire to do so. I enjoy living here because it allows me to be more in touch with God." He suddenly averted his gaze, most likely irritated.

Evdaimonia remarked, "If you answer my question, you will be released."

"I'd like you to clarify what this remark from the text means." He handed the book to me.

"He's the Masked Man from Heaven, as we've come to know him. I'm confident God won't take long to send him, so find him soon."

"Okay, I'll set you free. However, it is entirely up to you whether or not you wish to remain here." The prison was unlocked by Evdaimonia. "And let us know if you find him first."
